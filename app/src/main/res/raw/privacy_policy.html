Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 22, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how the PureTech Phone application (the “App”) provided by PureTech (“PureTech”, “we”, “us” or “our”) handles information. We have designed the App to keep your information on your device and under your control.

1. Our privacy approach: on-device by design

The App is a local telephone dialer designed to keep your data on your device. It does not operate user accounts and does not include third-party advertising or analytics software development kits, and it does not send your contacts, call log, recordings or other personal information to PureTech or to any server. The information the App works with is processed and stored locally on your device.

The single exception is an optional, manually-triggered “Check for updates” feature. When — and only when — you tap it, the App connects over the internet to GitHub (a third-party code- and release-hosting service operated by Microsoft) to look for a newer version of the App and, if you choose, to download the update file. This connection sends only the standard technical information that any internet request includes (such as your device’s IP address and request headers) to GitHub; it does not transmit your personal data, contacts, call log or recordings. GitHub’s handling of that request is governed by its own privacy policy, which we do not control. If you never use the update feature, the App makes no network connections.

2. Information the App accesses on your device

To function as a phone app, the App accesses certain information on your device through the permissions you grant. This information is used only on the device to provide the App’s features and is not transmitted to us:

  • Phone and call state — to place, receive and manage calls and to show call status.
  • Call log — to display your recents and per-number call history and totals, and to add or remove entries.
  • Contacts — to match numbers to names, search contacts and show caller information.
  • Microphone — used only when you choose to record a call, so the system can capture call audio.
  • Storage / files — to save call recordings into your device’s Music folder and organize them into a sub-folder.
  • Notifications — to show incoming, ongoing and missed-call notifications.
  • Bluetooth — to show the name of a connected Bluetooth audio device during a call.
  • Internet — used only when you tap “Check for updates”, to contact GitHub and download a newer version of the App. It is not used to transmit your personal data.

3. Call recordings

If you use the optional call-recording feature, the recording is produced by your device or operating system and saved as an audio file on your device’s storage (for example, in the Music folder). Recordings are stored locally and are not uploaded to PureTech. You are responsible for the recordings you create, for storing them securely, and for complying with all laws that apply to recording calls, including obtaining any consent required from other parties (see our Terms of Service). You can listen to, move, share or delete your recordings using your device’s file or media apps.

4. Information we collect about you

PureTech does not collect, receive, store or sell your personal information, your contacts, your call log or your recordings. PureTech operates no server that receives your data. The App’s only network activity is the optional update check described in Section 1, which contacts GitHub to retrieve release information and, at your choice, the update file; that request is handled by GitHub under its own privacy policy and does not transmit your personal data to PureTech. We do not build profiles about you and we do not share your information with advertisers or data brokers.

5. Third parties

Placing and receiving calls necessarily involves your mobile carrier and your device’s operating-system vendor, which operate the underlying telephone, network and recording capabilities. Their handling of your information is governed by their own privacy policies, which we do not control. If you choose to share a recording or other content using another app, that other app’s privacy practices apply.

6. Security

Information handled by the App stays on your device and is protected by your device’s own security (such as your screen lock and the operating system’s app sandbox). No method of storage is completely secure; you are responsible for securing your device and for protecting any recordings or exported files you create.

7. Data retention and your control

Because information is stored on your device, it remains there until you remove it or uninstall the App. You can delete call-log entries within the App, delete recordings through your file or media apps, revoke permissions in your device settings, and uninstall the App at any time, which removes its locally stored settings.

8. Children’s privacy

The App is not directed to children under the age of 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your jurisdiction), and we do not knowingly collect information from children. The App should be used in accordance with the eligibility requirements in our Terms of Service.

9. Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights regarding personal information under laws such as the GDPR or the CCPA/CPRA. Because PureTech does not collect or hold your personal information, the practical way to exercise control is through your device: you can access, correct, export and delete the information stored locally, and manage the App’s permissions, at any time. If you have questions about your rights, contact us using the details below.

10. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The updated version will be effective when made available within the App or otherwise published, and the “Last updated” date above will change accordingly. Your continued use of the App after an update constitutes acceptance of the revised Policy.
